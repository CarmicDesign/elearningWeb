Around the world in 30 minutes
Designing and developing modules in Articulate 360
Project year
2023
Duration
5 +
weeks
Tools used
Storyline, Ai, Ae, Ps & Pr

Introduction

The purpose of this case study is to highlight the self-exploration journey of designing and developing a game-like course titled "Around the World in 30 Minutes" using Articulate Storyline 360. The course aimed to provide an engaging and interactive experience for learners to explore different countries and cultures. The structure involved visiting each country, accessing country-specific information, completing quizzes, unlocking mini-games, and earning visas to travel to the next destination.

Course Structure

The course was divided into several modules, each representing a different country. It lets learners to start their journey in one country and progressed to the next after successfully completing a quiz. The content for each country included key information, cultural insights, landmarks, and interesting facts. The gamification elements, such as unlocking mini-games and earning visas, added a sense of achievement and motivation to the learning experience.

Functionality

Articulate Storyline 360 provided a robust authoring environment, allowing for the implementation of advanced triggers, variables, and JavaScript to enhance the interactivity and functionality of the course.

Navigation
Triggers and variables were employed to enable learners to navigate through the course, access information, and progress to the next country once the quizzes were successfully completed.
Gamification
Advanced triggers and variables enabled the unlocking of mini-games as learners progressed through the course. These mini-games provided additional engagement and a break from the informational content, allowing for a more enjoyable learning experience.
Personalisation
By leveraging variables, learners could revisit the course from different checkpoints, allowing them to explore different countries and paths based on their preferences. This personalisation aspect increased learner engagement and added replay value to the course.

Information Architecture

To ensure a seamless learning experience, a comprehensive information architecture was implemented. Each country module had a consistent layout, making it easy for learners to navigate and access the required information. Clear navigation buttons, progress indicators, and interactive elements were strategically placed to guide learners through the course. The course's overall design and layout were optimized for both desktop and mobile devices to accommodate different learning preferences.

Features

By employing complex logic within Articulate Storyline 360, the course "Around the World in 30 Minutes" achieved a seamless and user-friendly experience while maintaining a focus on the main concepts. The use of complex logic allowed for the implementation of features that enhanced the learning journey:

Progress Tracking
Advanced triggers and variables were utilised to track learners' progress throughout the course. This included keeping track of completed quizzes, unlocked mini-games, and visas earned. Progress indicators were integrated into the course interface, enabling learners to visualise their advancement and stay inspired.
Adaptive Feedback
Complex logic was employed to provide adaptive feedback based on learners' quiz performance. Depending on their answers, learners received customised feedback that guided them towards the correct understanding of the concepts. This adaptive feedback approach ensured that learners had a tailored learning experience that addressed their specific needs.
Contextual Navigation
The course incorporated logic to provide contextual navigation options. Learners were presented with relevant choices for exploration based on their current country or progress. This approach allowed learners to dive deeper into specific topics of interest while maintaining a coherent learning flow.
Conditional Branching
To create a personalised experience, the course employed conditional branching using advanced triggers and variables. Learners were presented with different content or paths based on their previous choices or performance. This adaptive branching ensured that learners received targeted content and reinforcement of key concepts.
Error Handling
Complex logic was utilized to handle errors and unexpected user interactions. The course anticipated potential errors or invalid inputs and provided clear instructions or prompts to guide learners towards the correct actions. This error handling approach minimized confusion and frustration, maintaining a smooth user experience.
Mastery-based Learning
The course integrated complex logic to offer mastery-based learning opportunities. Learners who demonstrated a high level of understanding and performance were presented with optional advanced challenges or bonus content to further deepen their knowledge. This approach encouraged learners to strive for excellence and provided an additional layer of engagement.

Conclusion

By incorporating complex logic into the course design, "Around the World in 30 Minutes" achieved an easy and seamless user experience. Learners were able to navigate through the content intuitively, receive personalised feedback and guidance, and explore additional challenges based on their individual progress and preferences.

Overall, the self-exploration journey on Articulate Storyline 360 showcased the successful integration of complex logic with in-depth information architecture, engaging visuals, and game-like elements. This resulted in a highly interactive and immersive learning experience that captivated learners while ensuring a solid understanding of the main concepts.
